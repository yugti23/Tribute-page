Early Life

Mahatma Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India. He studied law in London and later became a civil rights activist in South Africa, where he developed his philosophy of non-violent resistance, known as Satyagraha.

Career Highlights

  • Leader of Indian Independence Movement: Gandhi played a pivotal role in mobilizing the masses against British rule through non-violent protests and civil disobedience.
  • Salt March (1930): This 240-mile march to the Arabian Sea to protest the British salt tax became a significant event in the struggle for independence.
  • Champion of Non-Violence: Gandhi's philosophy of non-violence inspired civil rights movements around the world, influencing leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.

Awards and Recognitions

  • Time Magazine’s Person of the Year (1930)
  • Nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize multiple times (never awarded)
  • Global recognition as a symbol of peace and non-violence

International Recognition

  • Globally respected figure for his role in non-violent protest and civil rights.
  • His principles have influenced numerous movements for social change worldwide.

Contributions Beyond Politics

Gandhi was also a prolific writer and thinker, penning works such as "The Story of My Experiments with Truth." His writings reflect his philosophy of life, spirituality, and ethics.

Personal Life

Gandhi was married at the age of 13 to Kasturba Gandhi and had four children. He led a life of simplicity, often wearing only a dhoti and shawl. His dedication to non-violence and truth was evident in all aspects of his life.

Legacy

Mahatma Gandhi's legacy continues to resonate globally. His teachings on peace, non-violence, and civil rights remain influential, and his birthday, October 2nd, is celebrated as International Day of Non-Violence.

Quotes

“Be the change that you wish to see in the world.” – Mahatma Gandhi

“In a gentle way, you can shake the world.” – Mahatma Gandhi
